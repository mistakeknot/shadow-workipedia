# Iatrogenic Medical Harms

## Overview

Iatrogenic medical harms describes a systemic failure mode where a healthcare system designed to heal becomes a major source of injury and death through errors, infections, overtreatment, and automated decision failures. The key dynamic is institutional: overloaded care environments and perverse incentives amplify harm while suppressing learning and accountability.
As harms become visible and remediation fails, trust collapses, utilization patterns change, and the systemâ€™s ability to deliver safe care degrades further, creating a reinforcing loop.

## Game Mechanics

**Cascading Effects:**

- **Capacity Stress** -> **Error Rates**: Overload and staffing gaps increase mistakes, infections, and adverse drug events, worsening trust and utilization.
- **Automation Bias** -> **Accountability Vacuum**: AI triage and decision-support shift responsibility away from humans, complicating remediation when harm occurs.

## Warning Signs

- Rising adverse-event reports and hospital-acquired infection indicators alongside staffing shortages
- Evidence of systemic denial, underreporting, or retaliation against safety reporting
- Rapid expansion of clinical automation without validation or auditability
